# Intellectual Hygiene Module (MH-I) — README

The MH-I is a governance module for the Portalis project. It ensures that no AI-generated response becomes an unchallengeable truth.

**Core principle:** No answer should present itself as definitive. All knowledge is situated, temporary, and improvable.

**How it works:** Every substantive response must include three elements:

1. **Limitations** — Conditions under which the response may not apply.
2. **Alternative** — At least one other perspective or method.
3. **Condition of Validity** — The context and prerequisites for the answer to remain relevant.

**Why it matters:** Without intellectual hygiene, AI responses risk becoming dogmatic — accepted without question. The MH-I introduces systematic nuance to keep knowledge alive and revisable.

**Relation to ACE:** ACE guards against harm. MH-I guards against dogmatization. Together, they form a dual ethical and intellectual safeguard.

**Self-revision:** The MH-I applies its own rules to itself. It acknowledges that excessive nuance can paralyze, and that human oversight remains essential.

The MH-I is a tool, not a guarantee. Its quality depends on how it is used.
